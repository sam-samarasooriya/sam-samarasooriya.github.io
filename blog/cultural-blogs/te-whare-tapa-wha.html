Te Whare Tapa Whā

What does this mean?

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori health model developed by leading Māori health advocate Sir Mason Durie in 1984. Te Whare Tapa Whā shows us our wellbeing in four dimensions, and while it provides a Māori perspective on health, there is something in it for everyone, no matter who you are or where you are from. Te Whare Tapa Whā describes wellbeing as a wharenui or meeting house. The four walls of the wharenui represent the four dimensions of your holistic wellbeing.

Four Dimensions

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

    • "Taha tinana," or physical wellbeing, refers to the state of one's physical health and fitness. It encompasses various aspects, including:

    • Exercise:Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy body, improves cardiovascular health, and enhances mental well-being.
    • Nutrition:A balanced diet rich in nutrients supports overall health, energy levels, and immune function.
    • Sleep:Quality sleep is crucial for recovery, mental clarity, and overall physical health.
    • Hydration:Staying well-hydrated is vital for bodily functions and maintaining energy.

  • Taha Hinengaro (Mental Wellbeing)

    • Taha Hinengaro refers to mental wellbeing and encompasses emotional, psychological, and social aspects of health.

    • Emotional Awareness:Recognizing and understanding your feelings, which can help manage stress and relationships.
    • Resilience:Developing coping strategies to handle life's challenges and bounce back from adversity.
    • Social Connections:Building and maintaining healthy relationships, which provide support and enhance feelings of belonging.
    • Mindfulness and Self-Care:Engaging in practices that promote relaxation and self-reflection, such as meditation or hobbies.
    • Community and Relationships:Building supportive relationships that nurture spiritual growth and shared beliefs.

  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

    • Taha Wairua refers to spiritual wellbeing, which encompasses a sense of connection, purpose, and meaning in life.

    • Connection to Nature:inding peace and inspiration in the natural world, which can enhance spiritual experiences.
    • Cultural Identity: Embracing one's cultural heritage and traditions, which can provide a sense of belonging and identity.
    • Purpose and Meaning:Reflecting on personal values and goals, which can guide life choices and foster a sense of fulfillment.

  • Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

    • Taha Whānau refers to family wellbeing, emphasizing the importance of relationships, support systems, and the collective health of families and communities.

  • Healthy Relationships:Building strong, supportive connections within the family that foster trust, communication, and love.
  • Support Networks:Engaging with extended family and community to create a broader support system for individuals.
  • Cultural Values:Honoring and practicing cultural traditions and values that strengthen family identity and cohesion.
